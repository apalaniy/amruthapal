Project Overview

I worked with the UX Design team that services Expedia's Global Customer Operations team. I helped develop the Expedia virtual assistant by designing how the customer and chatbot interact with each other, assessing how the bot should handle customer questions after booking, and bringing the assistant to life through prototyping. My main work focused on three different product features: loyalty and rewards programs, authentication processes, and query completion.

 

The greater purpose behind Expedia's virtual assistant is to increase customers' self service capabilities, enabling them to perform tasks like cancel bookings, inquire about their rewards, and more. Through my work during the summer, I helped increase the number of skills that the virtual assistant can help a customer with, without having to defer to a physical agent. Some of the end results were a marked decrease in number of loyalty points related questions to physical agents and a significant increase in number of customers who were able to successfully authenticate.

 

Process Overview

Here's a glimpse of what my process to tackle these projects looked like. Due to NDA restrictions, I can't go into the details here, but if you'd like to learn more - feel free to email me at apalaniy@andrew.cmu.edu!

 

  1. Requirements Gathering | Understanding the why.
  2. Landscape Research | Understanding the who, what, when, where.
  3. Ideation| Brainstorming a wide range of solutions.
  4. Iteration | Turning ideas into high fidelity mock ups.
  5. Testing | Identifying the flaws and stengths of our solutions.
  6. Finalizing Phase 1 & North Star Designs | Iterating based on testing feedback.
  7. Product Reviews | Pitching designs for executive board approval.
  8. Phase 1 Launch | Working with eng & prod to start shipping solutions.

 

Team

I worked within a team of 16 UX deisgners. I also worked in small product feature oriented teams with product managers, content strategists, and tech leads.

 

Tools

Some tools I used were Sketch for mock ups, Zeplin and Invision for prototyping, Gliffy for interaction flow diagrams, and Outlook/Confluence/Jira for work management/ticket related issues.

 

Reflection

My big deliverables included presentations at company wide product reviews and final designs to be shipped as product features. From my experience at Expedia, I learned how to work in a fast paced environment with design and non design thinkers, developed strong visual and interaction design skills, and how to effectively present my designs and design process.

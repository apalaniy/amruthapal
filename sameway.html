Overview

My Sameway is a peer to peer ridesharing platform that makes traveling outside the city to spend a day hiking or at the beach easier for those who lack the means to do so. My challenge for the summer was to redesign the mobile application to have a simpler, safer, and easier user experience.

After having identified potential riders and their needs, my focus shifted to the drivers that were going to be using the platform. Working with the CEO, I recruited drivers to start creating and going on trips with riders on the platform, here's what the process looked like.

 

 

 

Findings

My main findings highlighted that the mobile platform was very confusing to navigate for current drivers. They often indicated confusion at terminology, pricing and tasks that simply took too long or were too convoluted. Some specific pain points are listed below.

 

  1. Users took, on average, 7 minutes to register their car. Only 40% of users were able to successfully register their car.
  2. 4 out of the 5 participants were unable to tell whether or not the car registration process had worked, indicating a lack of feedback.
  3. Users expressed confusion regarding how they were supposed to create trips. Trip creation success rate was 20%, a mere one user!

 

Design

Sitting down with the lead UX Designer, we re-evaluated our style guide. Based off my findings from my user research, I suggested we opt for lighter colors as the primary color palette and darker colors as highlights in order to create a more welcoming atmosphere. Usability Testing

Context

After creating the initial set of designs for the beta launch of the app, I wanted to conduct an additional round of usability testing. To get quick results, I skipped going through usertesting.com and took to the streets of Boston myself, approaching locals at a nearby coffeeshop to partake in our usability test.

 

 

Process

We asked participants to complete three tasks and recorded eye and hand movement during the testing sessions. We asked them to think out loud during the session and were able to gain insights as to customer pain points while using the mobile app.

 

 

 

Findings

The Good

Trip creation success rate went from 20% to 85%, indicating that the design improvements made it easier for users to create trips. On average, the time used to register a car was 7 minutes. This number dropped down to a whopping 1 minute 43 seconds seconds. The car registration flow rate of success increased from 40% to 90%. Overall, the numbers demonstrated that reducing the number of clicks and embracing a minimalistic design helped users navigate the platform in a more nimble and successful manner

 

The Bad

The results of the test also indicated a general lack of accessibility ("can I bring my bike, dog, etc), confusion between what the pages "day trips" and "create a trip" were each meant for ("what does day trips mean, does it mean I can take this trip?"), and concern for customer safety in a peer to peer platform ("It sounds scary to go on a day long trip with someone I don't know").

Reflection

Redesigning the entirety of a platform product was challenging, to say the least.

  1. First, I learned exactly how hard it is to design a peer to peer platform meant to be used by both service providers and service users. The nuances are endless and reducing clutter is key!
  2. Second, working in a small fast paced team at a high energy start up, I understood the importance of defining roles and setting boundaries. There were times where I had to use extensive rational, evidence, and point to user research we had conducted in order to convince tech leads that we needed to implement a certain feature in a certain way. Yet, there were also other times where I had to comporomise in order to ship a feature on time.
  3. Lastly, and most importantly, it became evident to me that as a designer you always have to reach for the moon so that you at least land amongst the starts. While it was not possible to launch certain product features immediately due to technical constraints, it was extremely productive to design the most ideal experience so that the team can always have it in mind, even if we couldn't ship it just yet.

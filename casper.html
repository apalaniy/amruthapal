Reflection

Conversational user interfaces can benefit users in situations where their hands and/or their visual attention are not available, such as when driving a car or cooking in a kitchen. They enable users to use language as an user interface medium to start interactions with systems where ever they want. Designing conversation required me to recognize the capabilities of the audio modality and limitations of human working memory. Through this project, I realized that designing interactions for such devices works best when the focus is purposeful and narrow. Creating value in such devices also required me and my team to identify problems worth solving. Next steps for my work in designing such interactions is to further my understanding of how natural language processing technology works, as well as system interactions between autonomous vehicles and CUI's.

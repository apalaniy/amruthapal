Overview

Our team worked with our client, the Greater Community Pittsburgh Foodbank to redesign their website for both mobile and web. The primary goal was to cater to the critical needs of the food bank's large, diverse & low-access target audience. Our solution sought to help our users quickly find food resources available to them, making their lives just a tad bit easier.

The Process

Who were our users? What were their needs? How could we help improve their quality of life? These are the questions that drove our process. Over the course of 12 weeks, we went through a series of research, testing, and design phases. Ultimately, our website redesign focused on making our users feel valued and connecting them to the resources most helpful to them.

Reflection

Food insecurity is a BIG problem. Thousands of Pittsburgh residents are facing a food shortage everyday. Tackling a website redesign for the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank meant first and foremost understanding the space and connecting to a type of user that may not even have access to technology on a regular basis. Whether it was at community kitchens, shelters, or pantries, our interactions with our users drove our designs. Literacy rates and educational attainment rates were in low 30s for our target audience. Poverty rates were through the roof. My biggest takeaway from this project was extracting user needs using out of the box research methods and translating those needs into our designs.

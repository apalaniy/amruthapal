I'm a double major at Carnegie Mellon University, I study both Economics and Human Computer Interaction. What does this mean? It means I always approach a problem from two different angles. My thought process closely follows design thinking principles, but my Economics background pushes me to think outside the box. Through my interdisciplinary background, I hope to bring a fresh perspective to every project I work on.

As a designer, I want to keep pushing the boundaries of familiarity, making people more comfortable with technology that exists outside of a screen. I'm curious as to how to better harness the power of web and mobile P2P platforms to improve quality of life. I'm obsessed with the co-creation of customer value in today's world, the idea that customers are always part of the process. As a UX designer, I want to be able to balance the needs of the stakeholder with the wants of the customer.

In my free time, you can find me dancing in studio, feasting on street food, or curled up with my dog and a good book. I'm always on the look out for good mystery novels or cool new design tools, hit me up if you know of any - or if you just wanna chat!

Usability Testing

Process

After creating the initial set of designs for the beta launch of the app, I wanted to conduct an additional round of usability testing. Our usability testing asked users to perform three tasks; create a trip, book a trip, and visit their trip history to file a complaint. To get quick results, I skipped going through usertesting.com and took to the streets of Boston myself, approaching locals at a nearby coffeeshop to partake in our usability test.

 

 

Findings

The results of the test indicated a general lack of accessibility, confusion between what the pages "day trips" and "create a trip" were each meant for, and concern for customer safety in a peer to peer platform.

Reflection

Reflection

Redesigning the entirety of a platform product was challenging, to say the least. First, I learned exactly how hard it is to design a peer to peer platform meant to be used by both service providers and service users. The nuances are endless and reducing clutter is key! Second, working in a small fast paced team at a high energy start up, I understood the important of defining roles and setting boundaries. There were times where I had to use extensive rational, evidence, and point to user research we had conducted in order to convince tech leads that we needed to implement a certain feature in a certain way. Yet, there were also other times where I had to comporomise in order to ship a feature on time. Lastly, and most importantly, it became evident to me that as a designer you always have to reach for the moon so that you at least land amongst the starts. While it was not possible to launch certain product features immediately due to technical constraints, it was extremely productive to design the most ideal experience so that the team can always have it in mind, even if we couldn't ship it just yet.

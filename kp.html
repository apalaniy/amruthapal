Overview

This is a case study of my redesign of the Hollar website for my Kleiner Perkins Fellows application. In my redesign, I focused on redesigning the home page to increase brand identity and incorporating a community reviews system to build trust in the brand based off of my research and user interviews. The challenge was to stretch the boundaries of familiarity without sacrificing customer experience.

Reflection

The redesign of web features for Hollar focused on better communicating brand identity and building trust in the dollar community. My first attempt at this redesign was fueled by observations made during the task analysis. The design solutions I put forth take a much needed step towards differentiating Hollar in an industry space that’s becoming quite saturated by companies like Wish and Shein and dominated by household names like Walmart and Amazon. The new homepage offers a sense of personalization, asking the user what they want and showcasing good deals without overwhelming them with a plethora of often meaningless categories. The new version of the search feature also heightens the feeling that Hollar is actually finding good deals for you and that the primary purpose of Hollar is to save customers money. Finally the reviews system is an essential component to any online ordering system that claims to save money without risking quality. Customers want to know what they’re getting and pictures + reviews do exactly that.

 

Next Steps

Seen above are simply the preliminary steps in tackling the redesign of the Hollar site.Given more time, there are other facets I would love to explore. Some steps I might take:

  1. The first step would be to conduct a 5 second test on the homepage to see how well Hollar’s brand identity is communicated at first sight. The test would also tell us if customers understand how Hollar is different and whether they would continue on to use the site.
  2. Next, I would go back to the drawing board to iterate on my current designs. What's working? What isn't?
  3. After that, I would test again! Prototype, Test, Iterate, Repeat!

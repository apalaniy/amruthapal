Reflection and Next Steps

Reflection

The redesign of web features for Hollar focused on better communicating brand identity and building trust in the dollar community. My first attempt at this redesign was fueled by observations made during the task analysis. The design solutions I put forth take a much needed step towards differentiating Hollar in an industry space that’s becoming quite populated by companies like Wish and Shein and dominated by household names like Walmart and Amazon. The new homepage offers a sense of personalization, asking the user what they want and showcasing good deals without overwhelming them with a plethora of often meaningless categories. The new version of the search feature also heightens the feeling that Hollar is actually finding good deals for you and that the primary purpose of Hollar is to save customers money. Finally the reviews system is an essential component to any online ordering system that claims to save money without risking quality. Customers want to know what they’re getting and pictures + reviews do exactly that.

 

Next Steps

The first step would be to conduct a 5 second test on the homepage to see how well Hollar’s brand identity is communicated at first sight. The test would also tell us if customers understand how Hollar is different and whether they would continue on to use the site. After that some additional usability testing might be conducted on the design of the review system as well as the rest of the site. Afterwards, I would go through multiple iterations of design and usability testing till arriving at a finalized design. Seen above is simply the preliminary steps in tackling the redesign of certain web features on the Hollar site.
